Art Projects

Trapped in Laughter

“Trapped in Laughter” shows a drawing of a seal dressed in as a little clown. The seal is presented inside a cage, representing its illiberality and forced to perform during events. By making the cage three-dimensional, I aimed to enhance its realism, allowing viewers to feel the sense of captivity more intensely. This piece highlights the challenges and restrictions that animals face in entertainment, prompting reflection on their well-being and the ethics of using them for performance.

Endless Hour

Endless hour’s 3D composition shows people working through the night, symbolizing the endless workload that many companies in China place on their employees. The clay figures are presented without light or paint, giving a more direct and pure representation of the intense work pressure. In this colorless, blank state, the figures lack individuality—no hobbies, no personal thoughts—only rules set by their bosses. Their health and well-being are disregarded. I have taken photos of the unpainted clay under different lighting, which highlights the exhaustion and the limits of the man’s body under the heavy workload.

Identity: Fear

The theme of the project is identity, presenting identity using soft materials. In my initial sketches, I drew things that reflect both my personal growth and the essence of who I am. Some of the sketches depict the distance between me and others, some symbolize the fears l've overcome, and some show my personality though using of colors. In the background, dream-like symbols represent past uncertainties. These drawings illustrate my journey from vulnerability to strength, showing how I've grown and what matters most.

Waves Within

The light box “waves within” capturing the magical moment when the sea enters in my room, filling it with marine life. The layers of paper and wood, lighted from behind shows a sense of fantasy. The dolphin, the fish, and soft swirl of seaweed gives a sense of calmness. Staying with these marine visitors, I wanted to suggest an embrace of nature's mysteries. The light box structure, with its shadows and gentle lighting it evokes a soft and dream-like setting where viewers can imagine themselves drifting between the known and the unknown.

Whispers of Inner Shadows

In this piece, I aim to communicate the complex relationship between inner fears and the illusions we create. By using mirrors to demonstrate viewers' reflections into a "ghost," I highlight how these fears can often appear as external threats, even though they originate from within us. The moving "ghost," enabled by an automaton mechanism, symbolizes the relentless pursuit of these fears, making them feel alive and dynamic. Ultimately, this work invites viewers to confront and reflect on their own self-imposed illusions.
